# ROI & Value Estimates — Procurement Intelligence Agent

Estimates based on system capabilities and semiconductor industry benchmarks.
Assumes a mid-to-large company with ~$500M annual component procurement spend.

---

## Value Drivers

### 1. Analyst Productivity — Most Concrete

A typical procurement decision cycle (pulling forecast data, checking inventory, running supplier comparisons, drafting a recommendation) takes **2–3 days manually**. This system compresses that to **minutes**.

- Team of 4 procurement analysts × 2 days/week saved = **~400 analyst-days/year**
- At a loaded cost of ~$120K/year per analyst ≈ **$400–500K/year in redirected capacity**
- Faster decisions also mean catching supply signals **weeks earlier** — which is the real payoff in semiconductors

---

### 2. Procurement Cost Savings — LP Optimization

The LP optimizer produces a **baseline comparison** on every run — showing the cheapest unconstrained plan vs. the risk-adjusted/diversified plan. Industry benchmarks for data-driven direct materials optimization:

- **3–8% reduction** on addressable procurement spend vs. ad-hoc/manual sourcing
- For a company with **$500M in annual component spend**: **$15–40M/year**
- The baseline comparison feature makes this delta visible and defensible to stakeholders on every decision

---

### 3. Working Capital — Inventory Optimization

The inventory optimizer surfaces trapped working capital on every run. At real scale:

- Reducing excess safety stock by **10–15%** without degrading service level
- For a firm carrying **$100M in component inventory**: **$10–15M freed**
- Lower carrying costs (~20–25% of inventory value annually): **$2–4M/year in carrying cost reduction**

---

### 4. Risk Avoidance — Largest but Hardest to Quantify

- A single **tier-1 supply disruption** costs **$5–50M in lost production per week**
- The system's diversification modes and geopolitical risk signals reduce single-supplier concentration
- Reducing disruption exposure from **15% to 8% annual probability** on a critical component line = **$3.5–10M expected value saved per year**

## Caveats

- The $30–65M range assumes a **mid-to-large company** (~$500M annual component procurement)
- Scales up significantly for Tier 1 OEMs — Intel, Apple, Samsung spend **$10B+** annually on components; value scales proportionally
- The LP baseline comparison built into the system means **cost savings are measurable per decision**, not just estimated in aggregate — ROI is trackable from day one
